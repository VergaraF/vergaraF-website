SnapHunt

SnapHunt is a mobile application for android devices. SnapHunt aims to change the way people interact with their cities, and gives business new tools to promote their products and services. It uses Google Maps to display interesting geopoints on a map given by users locations where they would go to and take a picture. The picture has to be something related to a theme given by the app, and the objective is for users to guess what the application wants them to take a picture of. Then, the SnapHunt would use Clarifai API to recognize the picture taken by the user, and tell him if he is correct or not by using image recognition. Our backend on PHP was used to allow people go online and compete, and see other people in the map as they are also snapping and hunting objects. A MySQL database was used to store our data.

SnapHunt was developed during ConUHacks, Concordia University Hackathon in Montreal, Quebec. Below you may find information about its tech stack and APIs used to make this possible.
